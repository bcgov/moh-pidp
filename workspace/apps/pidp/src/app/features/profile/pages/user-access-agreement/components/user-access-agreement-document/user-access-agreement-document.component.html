Access harmonization user access agreement

Purpose

This OneHealthID Service Use Policy Agreement (the "Policy Agreement") sets out your rights and responsibilities when you (the "User"):

  1. use the OneHealthID Service (the "Service");

  2. use the Service to access a system that contains health information (each a "System").

To use the Service, and to access a System, the User must acknowledge the acceptance of:

  1. this Policy Agreement;

  2. if required by a System Owner, a separate user agreement specific to the System.

Ownership

The Service is the property of His Majesty the King in right of the Province of British Columbia, as represented by the Minister of Health (the "Province").

The System(s) that can be accessed using the Service are the property of the Province or another health sector organization (each a "System Owner").

Commitment to Service and System Owner(s)

By using the Service, the User is deemed to enter a written contract with the Province based on the terms of this Policy Agreement.

By using the Service to access the System of a System Owner, the User is deemed to enter a written contract with that System Owner based on the terms of this Policy Agreement.

A System Owner may require that the User agrees to separate terms of use specific to the System being accessed ("Specific System Terms"). Such a requirement may be communicated by the System Owner to the User from time to time. In such situations, the Specific System Terms will prevail over the terms of the agreement described in the paragraph above to the extent of any conflict or inconsistency.

Acceptable Use Policy for the Service

  • I, the User, will:

    • use the Service only to access those System(s) necessary to perform the User's job duties, unless otherwise permitted in writing by the applicable System Owner(s);

    • protect the security, confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the Service;

    • use the Service in accordance with the applicable laws and any applicable policies of the Province relating to the Service which have been communicated by the Province to the User from time to time;

    • respect the rights and privacy of other Service users.

Terms of Use for System(s)

  • I, the User, will:

    • only access a System using the Service if the User:

      1. Is a registrant practicing a health profession designated under the Health Professions Act (British Columbia) or is a health professional designated under the similar act of another Canadian Province or Territory (a "Registrant") and is in good standing with the User's applicable regulatory college; or
      2. Is supervised by a Registrant, in good standing with their regulatory college, and is accessing the System on behalf of that Registrant; or
      3. Is neither a Registrant nor is supervised by a Registrant, and is providing services to the System Owner

    • only access and use a System using the Service

      1. as necessary to perform the User's work duties, unless permitted otherwise in writing by the System Owner; and
      2. in accordance with applicable laws and any applicable policies of the System Owner relating to the System which have been communicated by the System Owner to the User from time to time

    • protect the security, confidentiality, integrity, and availability of a System accessed using the Service, including as follows:

Breach Management: The User will cooperate with the execution of the System Owner's breach management plan including responding, reporting, and containing any potential or known breach of personal information including any unauthorized attempt, by any person, to access the System.

Breach Reporting: The User acknowledges that a System Owner(s) may report any material breach of this Policy Agreement to the Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner of British Columbia, and/or to the User's organization/supervisor.

Prevention of Unauthorized Access: The User will not permit any other person to access a System with the User's credentials and will take all reasonable measures to prevent unauthorized access from occurring.

Accuracy of Information: The User will make every reasonable effort to ensure to the best of the User's ability that any personal information User records in a System about the User or others, is accurate, complete, and up to date.

Reporting Inaccuracies: The User will notify the System Owner(s) of any inaccuracy or errors recorded in a System.

Audit Cooperation: The User will cooperate with any incident/privacy audits and investigations conducted by a System Owner(s).

Not use or disclose personal health information or information related to a health service Provider for the purpose of gaining insight into health trends, assessing the viability of a new product or service, etc. for monetary purpose or gain. That is, not for commercial use.

General

The Province may amend this Policy Agreement at any time, subject to the following:

  1. The OneHealthID Service will notify the User of any such amendment in advance by sending an email to the email address the User provided during Service enrolment. This email notice will include a description of the changes made and a link to the amended Policy Agreement.
  2. The amended Policy Agreement will take on the (the "Effective Date") in the email notice.
  3. Any continued use of the Service after the Effective Date constitutes the User's acceptance of the terms of the amended Policy Agreement.

The Province may terminate the User's access to the Service if the User fails to comply with this Policy Agreement or if the User's continued access represents an unforeseen risk to the Service, or if necessary due to a change in applicable law or policy, providing both reason and notice.

A System Owner may terminate the User's access to a System if the User fails to comply with this Policy Agreement in connection with the User's access to and use of that System or the User's continued access represents an unforeseen risk to the System, or if necessary due to a change in applicable law or policy providing both reason and notice at the System Owner discretion.

Definitions

  • Provider

    means a person who practices a designated health profession regulated by the Health Professions Act and is in good standing with their Professional College.

  • OneHealthID Services

    means the Province's suite of identity and access tools, including the platform where Providers and Supervised users may apply for access to Systems.

  • Supervised User

    means any member of a Provider's staff who is required to access a System in relation to the Provider's practice.

  • System

    means the various provincial or private computerized information systems available to access health information in BC.

  • System Owner

    means the Health Authority, Ministry or organization responsible and accountable for data stored within a System.
